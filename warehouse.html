Challenges of OEM-Designed Warehouse Automation Solutions


Getting a warehouse automation solution designed by an Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) instead of a consultant can present several challenges:


Limited Customization :OEMs often offer standardized solutions that may not fully align with your specific operational needs, leading to inefficiencies or suboptimal performance.

Lack of Holistic Approach: OEMs may focus primarily on their products rather than considering your entire warehouse workflow, potentially missing opportunities for end-to-end optimization.

Integration Issues: OEM-designed solutions might not integrate seamlessly with existing systems or equipment in your warehouse, leading to compatibility issues.

Higher Costs: OEMs might push proprietary systems that could result in higher initial costs and long-term expenses, especially if they are not scalable or flexible.

Limited Expertise in Process Optimization: Consultants bring broader industry experience and can provide valuable insights into improving warehouse processes, while OEMs might not have the same level of expertise in optimizing workflows.

Ongoing Support: OEMs might prioritize product sales over continuous support, leaving you with less guidance for post-installation maintenance and troubleshooting compared to a consultant-led solution.
